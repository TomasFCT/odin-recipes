Pandebono

What is a Pandebono?

Pandebono or pan de bono is a type of Colombian bread made of cassava starch, cheese, eggs, and in some regions of the country, guava jam. Traditionally, it is consumed with hot chocolate, still warm a few minutes after baking. It is very popular in the Colombian department of Valle del Cauca. This bread can be made in both a round and a ring shape.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup (250 g) of queso costeño (strong cheese made in Colombian Caribbean Coast) (250 g)
  • Half a cup cassava starch (130 g)
  • 4 tablespoons cornstarch (32 g)
  • 1 and a half tablespoons of sugar (24g)
  • A quarter of a teaspoon of baking powder (1g)
  • 1 egg (50g)
  • 3 tablespoons of liquid milk (45ml)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F) 10 minutes before baking.
  2. Process the queso costeño with the help of a blender or food processor until it is pureed, remove, and reserve in a bowl.
  3. Separately, in another bowl place the yucca starch, cornstarch, sugar, and baking powder and add this mixture to the cheese previously reserved, add the egg and knead vigorously until it has a smooth consistency, add milk if required, this depends on the humidity of the cheese.
  4. The ideal consistency of this preparation is like a very soft plasticine.
  5. Separate the dough into 10 portions and make small balls, place in a baking dish and bake for 20 minutes or until golden brown.
